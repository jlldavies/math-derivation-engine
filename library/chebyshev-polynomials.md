## The rule
The Chebyshev polynomial of the first kind `T_n` is defined by
`T_n(cos őł) = cos nőł`, i.e. `T_n(x) = cos(n arccos x)` on `[‚ąí1,1]`. It is a genuine
degree-`n` polynomial, generated by the three-term recurrence
`T_{n+1}(x) = 2x T_n(x) ‚ąí T_{n‚ąí1}(x)`, with `T_0(x) = 1`, `T_1(x) = x`.
The family is **orthogonal on `[‚ąí1,1]` under the weight `1/‚ąö(1‚ąíx¬≤)`**:
`‚ąę_{‚ąí1}^{1} T_m(x)T_n(x)/‚ąö(1‚ąíx¬≤) dx = 0` for `m ‚Č† n` (and `ŌÄ` for `m=n=0`, `ŌÄ/2`
otherwise) ‚ÄĒ the substitution `x = cos őł` turns this into the cosine orthogonality on
`[0,ŌÄ]`.

## Worked example
Take the recurrence one step: `T_2(x) = 2x¬∑T_1(x) ‚ąí T_0(x) = 2x¬∑x ‚ąí 1 = 2x¬≤ ‚ąí 1`.
Substituting `x = cos őł` gives `T_2(cos őł) = 2cos¬≤őł ‚ąí 1 = cos 2őł`, the double-angle
formula ‚ÄĒ confirming `T_2(cos őł) = cos 2őł`. One more step gives
`T_3(x) = 2x(2x¬≤‚ąí1) ‚ąí x = 4x¬≥ ‚ąí 3x`, i.e. `cos 3őł = 4cos¬≥őł ‚ąí 3cos őł`. Standard results
(Rivlin, *Chebyshev Polynomials*, ch. 1).

## Explain (altitudes)
- **expert** ‚ÄĒ `T_n` are the eigenfunctions of the singular Sturm‚ÄďLiouville operator
  `(‚ąö(1‚ąíx¬≤)(‚ąö(1‚ąíx¬≤)u')')' + n¬≤u = 0`; the map `x = cos őł` conjugates them to the
  cosine basis, so orthogonality, the `2^{1‚ąín}` minimax (equioscillation) property and
  the fast cosine-transform evaluation all descend from Fourier analysis on `[0,ŌÄ]`.
- **working** ‚ÄĒ de Moivre expands `cos nőł` as a polynomial in `cos őł`; collecting it
  defines `T_n(x)`, and the cosine addition formula
  `cos(n+1)őł + cos(n‚ąí1)őł = 2cos őł cos nőł` is exactly the recurrence. Orthogonality is
  the cosine integral after `x = cos őł`.
- **plain** ‚ÄĒ there's a polynomial that turns "cosine of `n` times an angle" into a
  formula in plain `cos őł`. You build the next one from the previous two by
  `T_{n+1} = 2x T_n ‚ąí T_{n‚ąí1}`, starting from `1` and `x`.
